Library Rules & Regulations

N. J. Yasaswy Memorial Library

General Rules

  1. Library is primarily intended for the use of faculty, scholars, staff and students of IFHE. Outsiders may be allowed to avail of the facilities for reference purpose only, subject to the approval of the Director.
  2. Personal belongings (like briefcase, umbrella, personal books, bags, etc.) are not allowed into the library.
  3. Silence shall be strictly observed in the library.
  4. Smoking is not allowed in the Library premises.
  5. No eatables are allowed into the library.
  6. Users should come in formal dress.
  7. A user shall be responsible for any damage done by him/her to the documents of library. He/she will be required to replace such documents.
  8. Books taken from the shelves for reference should be left on the table. Users should not replace the books in the shelves.
  9. Users can reserve books in advance with the Librarian.
  10. Books should be handled with care. No markings of any kind with pen or pencil should be made in any book or document.
  11. Books borrowed should be returned in good condition. If a book is found with pages missing/torn/mutilated etc., the borrower should pay the cost of the book. If the borrowed book is lost, the user concerned will be held responsible for replacing the same or pay the cost of the book as per present market price.
  12. After reading, users are expected to fold the newspapers properly and put them in the places earmarked for them.

Guidelines for Circulation of Documents

Issue of Books to Students

  1. Each student can borrow upto a maximum of two books against his / her card for a period of seven days.
  2. A book may be renewed for a further period of seven days, provided the book has not been reserved by other users. Books have to be produced in the library counter for renewal.
  3. If a book is returned after the due date, Rs.2/- shall be levied as fine per day upto seven days, and Rs.5/- per day beyond 7 days.
  4. Reference books will not be issued to the students for reading at home. However, students are free to refer such books in the library.
  5. If a book is overdue, no book will be issued against and until the overdue book is returned and fine remitted.
  6. If a student does not return a book even after three successive reminders of every five days, he/she is liable to pay the cost of the book.
  7. No student is permitted to borrow magazines/journals.

Issue of Books to Faculty

  1. A faculty member can borrow upto a maximum of ten books against his / her card for a period of 15 days. Books have to be produced in the library for the renewal.
  2. A book may be renewed for a further period of 15 days, provided that the same has not been reserved by other user.
  3. If a book is overdue, no book will be issued against and until the overdue book is returned.
  4. A faculty member has to return all borrowed books to the library at the time of stock verification. If he/she fails to return the books, library will treat such books as lost and the details of such books will be forwarded to accounts department for recovery of the lost.
  5. No magazines/journals will be issue.

Issue of Books to Research Scholars

  1. A Scholar can borrow upto a maximum of six books against his / her card for a period of 15 days.
  2. A book may be renewed for a further period of 15 days, if no other user has reserved these books. Books have to be produced in the library for renewal.
  3. If a book is overdue, no book will be issued against and until the overdue book is returned.
  4. No magazines/journals will be issued.

Issue of Books for Staff

  1. A staff member can borrow two books against his / her card for a period of 15 days.
  2. Books can be renewed for a further period of 15 days, provided no other user has reserved these books. Books have to be produced in the library counter for renewal.
  3. If a book is overdue, no book will be issued against and until the overdue book is returned.
  4. No magazines/journals will be issued.
The Library Administration reserves the right to modify rules and regulations whenever necessary.
